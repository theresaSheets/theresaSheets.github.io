Math Modeling to Imporove Lives

I'm a PhD Data Scientist based in Salt Lake City, Utah. My research lies at the intersection of mathematics, biology, and computer science. I develop mathematical models of disease to examine phenomena difficult to study experimentally. I am drawn to projects where the illumination provided by modeling and mathematical analysis can inform simple solutions to save lives. At the University of Utah, I was a member of the Ecology, Immunology, and Epidemiology Group.

Outside of work, I enjoy the getting out of the city to camp, climb, and ski.
